01 · Brand Essence

A trustworthy source for European salary data.

EuroSalary.eu turns opaque European pay markets into clear, comparable, actionable salary intelligence — for workers, employers, and policy makers.

Our visual language sits at the intersection of European institutional authority and modern fintech clarity. Serious enough to be trusted with compensation decisions. Clean enough to feel useful, not bureaucratic.

01

Transparent

We show the methodology, the sample size, the date. No pay data without provenance.

03

Actionable

A salary index is only useful if it moves a decision. Negotiate, hire, relocate.

08 · Voice & Tone

Authoritative, never bureaucratic.

We sound like a trusted analyst at a central bank — not like a government circular, and not like a startup pitch. Clear, numeric, European.

We say

  • "Median software engineer salary in Luxembourg: €72,400 (Q1 2026, n=1,284)."
  • "Data from 27 EU member states."
  • "Negotiate with evidence."
  • "Methodology transparent — see our calculation."
  • "Across sectors, seniority and geography."

We don't say

  • "Amazing salary data you won't believe!"
  • "Pursuant to Directive 2019/1152/EU..."
  • "Unlock your earning potential today."
  • "Salary insights, game-changed."
  • "According to our sources..." (always cite, never hedge)

9.2 · Brand archetype

Primary archetype
The Sage

We exist to bring truth and clarity to a market full of confusion. We don't sell. We don't motivate. We inform. Like the BBC, Reuters, or the IMF — what matters is being right and being clear.

peer brands: Reuters · Eurostat · IMF · Bloomberg
Secondary archetype
The Citizen

We belong to all 27 EU countries equally. We respect every language, every market, every wage tradition. We are of the people, for the people — never preachy, never American.

peer brands: BBC · ARTE · Le Monde · Der Spiegel

9.7 · Naming & spelling

EuroSalary (one word, CamelCase) — never "Euro Salary", "Eurosalary", or "EUROSALARY".
EuroSalary.eu in writing — the .eu is part of the brand, not just a domain.
The European Salary Index — capital T, capital E, capital S, capital I.
EU-27 with hyphen — never "EU 27" or "EU27".
Q1 2026 — never "1Q26", "Q1/2026", "first quarter of 2026".
€72,400 — euro symbol before, comma as thousands separator (UK convention for the global English version).
Median, not "average". Use "P50" for technical contexts, "median" for everything else.

9.8 · Personality — yes / no

EuroSalary IS
  • Calm, confident, well-sourced
  • European in spirit and in metric system
  • Generous with methodology, never with hype
  • A friend who's read every report
  • Numeric where possible, plain where helpful
  • Politely skeptical of round numbers
  • Updated, dated, signed
EuroSalary IS NOT
  • A motivational coach
  • A LinkedIn influencer
  • A government bulletin
  • An American startup
  • A growth-hacker
  • A tabloid headline
  • An emoji factory
